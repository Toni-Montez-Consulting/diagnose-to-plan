# Research Arm Digest - Pattern Intelligence And Company Pattern Study

Date: 2026-05-15
Reviewer: Toni
Status: draft

## Digest Summary

The latest founder note extends the Glean / Business Memory OS insight into a
broader Research Arm pattern: Toni should study strong AI and workflow
companies to extract what they prove about work, then translate those patterns
into practical consulting assets. The goal is not to copy startups or chase
tools. The goal is to build a standing Pattern Intelligence loop that asks what
pain a company solved, what workflow it collapsed, what part is transferable to
small businesses, and what artifact Toni should create from the insight.

## What Changed

- The Glean insight is no longer only about business memory. It now suggests a
  repeatable research method.
- Toni named a possible internal function: Pattern Intelligence.
- The Research Arm should study companies such as Glean, Granola, Cursor,
  Lovable, Harvey, Sierra, n8n, Lindy, and Gamma as pattern sources.
- The useful output is not "AI news." The useful output is diagnostics,
  workflows, templates, offer ideas, client education, skills, checklists,
  runbooks, and future-phase ideas.
- The key filter is: every research note should answer, "What do I do with
  this?"

## Why It Matters

- This gives the practice a disciplined way to stay inventive without becoming
  tool-chasing or startup cosplay.
- It extends the Consulting Workspace OS: the practice brain should remember
  Toni's methodology, client patterns, delivery assets, and market
  observations.
- It supports a stronger moat: not generic AI consulting, but translation of
  market patterns into systems small businesses can actually run.
- It creates a bridge between Research Steward and Business Pattern Steward:
  research finds the signal; business-pattern extraction translates the
  signal into operator judgment.
- The first Glean company-pattern study now exists, so the next question is
  whether the study fields are useful enough to become a reusable template.

## Hype Filter

What is real:

- AI software companies are exposing useful patterns in enterprise search,
  meeting memory, vertical AI, customer experience, coding/build speed,
  workflow automation, and artifact generation.
- Toni's practice can learn from those patterns without copying the products.
- A manual research-to-artifact loop can sharpen diagnostics, offers, client
  language, and internal delivery systems.

What may be hype, premature, or not relevant:

- Building a universal SaaS or "small-business Glean" platform.
- Assuming venture-backed company success proves small-business willingness to
  pay.
- Treating company examples as public proof before primary-source review.
- Turning every interesting product into an offer, skill, or implementation
  project.
- Scheduled research automation before the manual loop proves value.

single-step assistance toward multi-step workflow infrastructure, but the
winning pattern for Toni's practice should stay human-led: agents gather,
synthesize, recommend, and draft; Toni approves authority, public language,
pricing, client scope, and implementation. This supports a Research Arm V0 now
and an Opportunity OS V0 next.

## What Changed

- Toni's agent-system idea sharpened from "more specialized agents" into a
  practical operating model: continuous research and recommendation with
  human-controlled authority.
- The Research Arm now has a clear first job: reduce random link chasing and
  turn external AI/software/workflow signals into practice updates.
- The consulting-growth idea sharpened into a separate Opportunity OS lane:
  relationship-led opportunity capture, referral mapping, fit scoring, and
  capacity protection.
- The AI agents report supports the idea that agents are moving into
  production, coding, reporting, internal process automation, and multi-step
  workflows. This fits Toni's builder/advisor positioning.

## Why It Matters

- Toni's consulting practice can differentiate through judgment, translation,
  sequencing, implementation, and handoff, not by sounding like an AI automation
  agency.
- A Research Arm helps Toni stay current without making every new tool or
  article into a distraction.
- The Research Arm can produce better client explanations: what matters, what
  is hype, what is safe to try, and what should wait.
- The Opportunity OS can later help Toni grow through trust networks without
  overcommitting or chasing low-fit work.

## Hype Filter

What is real:

- Multi-step AI workflows and coding agents are becoming mainstream enough to
  influence client expectations.
- Research/reporting and internal process automation are natural consulting
  wedge areas.
- Human oversight, governance, source quality, and implementation boundaries
  matter more as agent workflows become more capable.

What may be hype, premature, or not relevant:

- Fully autonomous agents changing business systems without owner approval.
- "Agentic" marketing language with no operating discipline.
- Tool-first positioning that ignores client context.
- Building a scheduled research platform before one manual digest loop proves
  useful.

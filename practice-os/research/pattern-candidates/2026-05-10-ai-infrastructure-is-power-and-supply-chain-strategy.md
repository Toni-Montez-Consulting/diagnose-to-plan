# Research Pattern Candidate - AI infrastructure is power and supply-chain strategy

- Candidate id: 2026-05-10-ai-infrastructure-is-power-and-supply-chain-strategy

## Observation

- What was observed: AI infrastructure is being discussed less as pure software
  and more as a physical operating system: power availability, data-center
  siting, utility load forecasts, permits, HBM/memory bandwidth, storage tiers,
  cooling, and supply-chain timing.
- Where it showed up: EIA and EPRI load-growth research, xAI Memphis/Colossus
  coverage, NVIDIA Vera Rubin architecture, and HBM4 vendor updates.
- Why it caught attention: Toni flagged energy expansion, xAI data centers,
  memory companies, NVIDIA, and power companies as research topics that should
  inform the practice.
- Who or what type of operator it may apply to: founders, small businesses, and
  operators evaluating AI adoption, especially when they assume AI is only a
  tool decision and not an operating-model decision.

## Underlying Principle

- What seems to be true: Useful AI is constrained by the system around it:
  infrastructure, data, cost, latency, review, ownership, and handoff.
- What business or human behavior is underneath it: People often want the
  visible capability before understanding the operating commitments behind it.
- What would make the principle false: If AI systems became effectively free,
  instant, universally reliable, low-energy, low-governance, and operationally
  simple enough that infrastructure and handoff no longer mattered.

## Consulting Translation

- How this changes discovery: Ask what the client wants AI to do, but also ask
  what inputs, decisions, review steps, costs, latency expectations, and human
  ownership the workflow requires.
- How this changes diagnosis: AI feasibility is not just "can a model answer
  this?" It is "can the business support the workflow around the model?"
- How this changes delivery or handoff: Prefer small, operable AI layers first:
  intake support, drafting, classification, review prep, follow-up, and admin
  acceleration before full autonomy or heavy custom AI surfaces.
- How this changes messaging or client education: Explain AI as leverage with
  an operating model, not magic software.
- Business Pattern Steward translation: Treat this as the first internal
  business-agent fixture. Research proves what is happening; Business Pattern
  Steward extracts the transferable lesson; Consulting Strategy decides whether
  the lesson should change discovery, Blueprint language, or client education.
- What Toni should watch for next time: Clients asking for AI features without
  data readiness, review ownership, cost expectations, or operational handoff.

## Observation

- What was observed: Soft launches, community posts, first-user waves, ad tests, and pilot pushes create valuable operating signal before they create public proof.
- Where it showed up: Consulting proof-readiness receipts, client operating kits, and the held Greg soft-launch note.
- Why it caught attention: Launch signal can guide product, support, copy, and proof decisions if captured before it gets flattened into "went well."
- Who or what type of operator it may apply to: Founders, small businesses, app builders, and local operators running early launches.

## Underlying Principle

- What seems to be true: Momentum is useful only when it is captured with evidence limits, caveats, and next decisions.
- What business or human behavior is underneath it: Excitement can make teams overclaim, while lack of capture makes real learning vanish.
- What would make the principle false: If the launch produced no observable signal or all signal is too private to record safely.

## Consulting Translation

- How this changes discovery: Ask what launch channel, audience, metric, feedback, and permission boundary exists.
- How this changes diagnosis: Separate product traction, channel traction, proof readiness, and operational workload.
- How this changes delivery or handoff: Record metrics, feedback, bugs, caveats, and next actions before changing strategy.
- How this changes messaging or client education: Teach clients to treat early launch feedback as operating intelligence, not vanity.
- What Toni should watch for next time: Screenshots with raw customer data, unsupported metrics, and "great launch" claims without denominator or caveat.

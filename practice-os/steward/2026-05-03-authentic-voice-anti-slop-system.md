# Steward Receipt: Authentic Voice And Anti-Slop System

## Trigger

Toni clarified that AI selling and positioning often reads as fake: punchy,
overly prosey, buzzword-heavy, and believable only to someone who does not
understand the work. The practice needs a durable standard so this preference
applies to websites, marketing materials, documents, emails, planning,
positioning, proof packets, and assistant outputs.

## Decision

DTP owns the canonical standard because DTP already owns Practice OS,
proof/redaction governance, templates, correction loops, and public-claim
review. Consulting should reference the DTP standard, not maintain a separate
voice doctrine.

## Implemented Surface

- Canonical policy:
  `practice-os/policies/authentic-voice-and-anti-slop.md`
- Audit template:
  `practice-os/templates/copy-authenticity-audit.md`
- Updated proof and public-claim templates with authenticity checks.
- Updated correction checklist with voice/positioning correction lanes.
- Updated consulting-intelligence evals with public homepage copy and client
  follow-up email anti-slop cases.
- Added an advisory copy scanner for obvious watchlist phrases.

## Operating Rule

The standard is not a word ban. It is a truthfulness and specificity gate.
The reviewer can keep a watched phrase only when the mechanism, evidence, and
caveat are clear.

## Public/Proof Gate

No public claim should move to Done unless it has:

- evidence;
- caveat;
- permission level;
- redaction status;
- reviewer;
- authentic wording that names what actually happened.

## Summary Read

DTP is well scoped as Toni's internal Practice OS and consulting spine. It
already handles intake, diagnosis, proof/redaction, client cadence, build
handoff, steward review, research digestion, Business Memory diagnostics,
Campaign Ops research, Business Admin planning, and offer inventory.

The uncovered layer is a normalized business-domain operating map. A real
business can fail in finance, sales, support, delivery, people/vendor
management, legal/procurement, reporting, training, launch adoption, or
domain-specific operations even when the consulting workflow itself is clean.

The new Business Operating System Coverage Map fills that gap without turning
the practice into public copy, software, automation, a SaaS idea, or a generic
AI offer.

## Gap Ranking

### P0 Gaps

| Gap | Why it matters | Current safe move |
|---|---|---|
| Finance / cashflow / accounting operating model | Money state, invoicing, AR/AP, tax cadence, margin, and subscription/vendor cost are not yet normalized beyond Business Admin planning and blocked QuickBooks. | Keep financials manual/export-only; create domain audit rows and mark QuickBooks unavailable until read-only connector rules are accepted. |
| Sales pipeline / CRM operating model | Intake and follow-up exist, but opportunity stages, proposal state, close/lost learning, referrals, and pipeline health are not yet one reusable lane. | Use the BOS audit before the next Greg/Cam/CCAAP follow-up and route gaps into private kits. |
| Customer success / support / retention | Launch and handoff exist, but post-launch health, support severity, onboarding, renewal, and adoption rhythms are thin. | Use Omnexus/Greg as safe known lanes for support and activation questions without copying private user data. |
| Domain-specific operations map | Active lanes depend on domain rules: nonprofit donations/membership, app subscriptions, SMB M&A, local service scheduling, fitness programming. | Add domain-specific ops to every BOS audit before proposing a generic workflow. |

### P1 Gaps

| Gap | Why it matters | Current safe move |
|---|---|---|
| Legal / procurement operating model | COI exists, but contract operations, procurement approvals, vendor terms, insurance prompts, and professional-review routing are not yet reusable. | Keep issue spotting only; route legal/professional work to owner/pro review. |
| People / vendor / capacity model | Delegation, contractor handoffs, access review, QA ownership, and capacity are not first-class yet. | Use role/access tables in audits before suggesting command rooms or automations. |
| KPI / management reporting cadence | Verification and proof are strong, but owner-facing weekly management metrics are not yet consistent across domains. | Recommend weekly operating memos before dashboards. |
| Adoption / training standard | Handoff/runbooks exist, but behavior change, training, and review cadence need a stronger pattern. | Add training/adoption gates to launch and Client Operating Kit reviews. |

### P2 Gaps

| Gap | Why it matters | Current safe move |
|---|---|---|
| Automation and dashboards | Useful later, but premature before manual audits prove repeated domain pain. | Keep hosted DTP, Hub, Notion, dashboards, and commands gated. |
| Connector expansion | QuickBooks, ad platforms, analytics, CRM, support inboxes, and billing systems carry privacy and authority risk. | Use manual exports, summaries, or unavailable markers first. |
| Public packaging | The coverage map could become sellable, but public naming before proof would overstate it. | Keep as internal offer component only. |

## Coverage Decision

- Create a canonical coverage map: yes.
- Create a reusable manual template: yes.
- Create software, command, schema, hosted workflow, Notion sync, Hub runtime,
  QuickBooks OAuth, or connector behavior: no.
- Promote to public consulting copy: no.
- Treat Business Memory OS and Campaign Ops as included lenses inside the
  broader coverage map: yes.

## No-Touch Boundaries

- No consulting public copy changed.
- No client communication sent or drafted from this audit.
- No Hub runtime, hosted DTP, Notion sync, QuickBooks, ad account, CRM, billing,
  App Store, production, or private-vault action authorized.
- No raw financials, payment records, customer/user records, private analytics,
  support inbox content, legal documents, credentials, or client-private facts
  were copied into tracked DTP docs.

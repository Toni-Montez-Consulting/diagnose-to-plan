# Client Roadmap One-Pager - Generic Candidate

Purpose: translate the internal control plane into a simple client-facing roadmap shape. This is a generic candidate, not a live client send.

## Current State

The current system has useful pieces, but the next step is to make the work easier to trust, operate, and hand off.

## What Matters First

Start with the smallest high-leverage improvement that reduces confusion, removes a launch or operating blocker, and gives the owner a clearer way to run the system.

## What We Are Building

- A focused first-pass improvement plan.
- The minimum working system or workflow needed for the current goal.
- A handoff note that explains what changed, how to use it, and what to watch.
- A short verification receipt showing what was checked.

## What We Are Not Building Yet

- A broad platform rebuild.
- Automated decisions that still need human judgment.
- Public claims, screenshots, or proof without approval.
- Extra dashboards or portals unless they solve a repeated operating problem.

## Client Inputs Needed

| Input | Owner | Needed by |
|---|---|---|
| Approved public copy, links, or source material | Client | Before final content or launch |
| Decision-maker review | Client | Before public launch or handoff |
| Access to the required deployment or operating account | Client or Toni | Before production verification |

## Timeline / Appetite

- Target work window: one focused work block for the first checkpoint.
- Appetite: small enough to complete, verify, and hand off without opening a broad rebuild.
- Review points: after the first working checkpoint and before any public launch or proof use.

## Cost

- Estimate or fixed price: set after the first diagnostic pass.
- Assumptions: scope stays focused; client inputs arrive on time; no new platform or compliance requirements are added midstream.

## Handoff

The client should leave with:

- a working path or decision-ready plan;
- a plain-language operating note;
- known remaining blockers;
- what to do next without Toni;
- what to ask Toni for if support is needed.

## Support Option

Support can be a light follow-up block for launch checks, owner questions, small fixes, or the next operating improvement.

## Internal Notes To Remove Before Sending

- No internal implementation inventory.
- No uncommitted-work language.
- No private client facts from another engagement.
- No unapproved proof, screenshots, metrics, or claims.
- No employer, W-2, conflict, or internal operating details unless explicitly relevant and client-safe.

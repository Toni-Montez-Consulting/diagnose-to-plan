# Steward Receipt - Consulting Strategy Source Policy Pilot

Date: 2026-05-10

Status: accepted internal strategy pilot

## Trigger

After the External Communications pilot turned source-backed AI/workflow
language into draft-only messaging, the next useful role pilot was Consulting
Strategy: how should that language shape offer logic, buyer fit, Blueprint
positioning, and public-copy boundaries?

## Decision Captured

AI should not become the public front-door category by itself.

The stronger offer strategy is workflow-first:

- lead with unclear ideas, broken workflows, and tech bottlenecks;
- route unclear AI/workflow questions to Blueprint;
- use Fast Track only for contained, low-risk AI/workflow implementation;
- keep Custom SOW tied to approved scope, review boundaries, and handoff;
- treat Growth, AI, and Operating Systems as practical operating leverage, not
  "AI transformation."

## What Worked

- The role translated source evidence into buyer-fit rules instead of copy.
- The Blueprint became the default route for unclear AI/workflow opportunities.
- The bad-fit filter became sharper: chatbot-only, random automation, and
  AI-without-workflow requests are not the target.
- Public-copy gates stayed intact.

## Operating Rule Reinforced

Consulting Strategy can use research and source-backed language to clarify the
offer model, but it cannot publish public positioning, invent proof, or commit
to pricing/ROI claims without Toni approval.

source-pack file. Keep it small: encode the three proven pilots first instead
of trying to model every future agent at once.

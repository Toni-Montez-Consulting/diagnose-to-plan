Create an internal communications pilot that translates the reviewed
AI/platform and business research artifacts into usable language for future
prospect replies, client education notes, and executive-friendly explanations.

The pilot stays draft-only and internal. It does not create a Gmail draft,
client communication, public site copy, or approved claim.

## What Worked

- The External Communications role converted source-backed research into
  clear, human language without treating external sources as proof.
- The drafting standard produced three useful levels: prospect reply,
  executive-friendly version, and client education note.
- The send checklist made the boundary explicit: draft-only until Toni reviews.

## Operating Rule Reinforced

External Communications can translate reviewed source evidence into language,
but it cannot send, publish, or imply proof by itself.

## Next Recommendation

Use this artifact the next time a real prospect or client asks about AI,
workflow improvement, or business operating systems. The next pilot should be
against a real email or follow-up, using the required five-part email summary
before drafting.

## Blocked Actions

This pilot does not approve:

- Gmail draft creation;
- client send;
- public consulting copy;
- public source claims;
- ROI or revenue claims;
- proof movement;
- legal/compliance claims;
- autonomous communications.

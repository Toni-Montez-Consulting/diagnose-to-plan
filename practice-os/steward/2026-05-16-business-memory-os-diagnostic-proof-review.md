## Summary Read

The Business Memory OS diagnostic is useful enough to keep as an internal
consulting lens. It should not become public copy, a named offer, a SaaS plan,
a Glean competitor, or a connected assistant yet.

The Northline fixture proves the diagnostic can create a concrete source map,
25 recurring questions, safety classifications, ranked opportunities, and a
safe first workflow recommendation for a fictional local-service business.

The Omnexus fixture adds a known-business test. It shows the same diagnostic
can work against a more complex founder-operated product without copying
private user records, App Store Connect screenshots, raw analytics, billing
data, or production secrets into tracked DTP docs.

The caveat is that Northline is not buyer proof. It is a demo-quality shape
test, not evidence that the market wants a named Business Memory OS offer.

## Known-Business Follow-On

The Omnexus fixture was added as the second diagnostic because Omnexus is a
known founder-operated product with real operating complexity and enough
tracked repo truth to stay source-backed.

The Omnexus diagnostic produced:

- a source map across product scope, subscriptions, onboarding, SOPs, support,
  vendor/admin, and reporting;
- 25 recurring founder, engineer, support, user, reviewer, and agent questions;
- a safety split of 7 search, 8 draft, 8 workflow, and 2 human-only questions;
- three ranked opportunities around release/support memory, billing/App Store
  explanation, and weekly founder operating memos;
- one safe first workflow: a read-only release and support memory cockpit.

This confirms the diagnostic can handle both a fictional local-service business
and a known internal product without widening into implementation.

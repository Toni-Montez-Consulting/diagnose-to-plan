# LinkedIn Long Draft

I am building a small consulting practice around a simple idea:

Most small businesses do not need AI first. They need an operating system.

Not enterprise software. Not another app to ignore. Not a chatbot pasted onto a
messy workflow.

I mean the practical system around how the business actually runs:

- how work comes in;
- who follows up;
- what gets tracked;
- what the owner has to remember;
- what can be handed off;
- what should stay manual;
- where AI can help without making the business harder to operate.

The operator is the unit. Sometimes that operator is the owner. Sometimes it is
a spouse, assistant, coach, admin, or founder. The work starts by understanding
what they are carrying.

My process is straightforward:

1. Diagnose the real bottleneck.
2. Plan what should happen first.
3. Translate the system into language the operator can own.
4. Build the first useful layer.
5. Hand it off with a runbook.
6. Stay close long enough to see what breaks.

Behind that, I am building a repo-centered Business Brain for the practice:
skills, command contracts, fixtures, agent roles, proof gates, and lessons that
compound over time.

The goal is not to make this SaaS. The goal is to make the work sharper every
time without losing the human judgment that makes the system useful.

Public proof and specific client names require review, so I am keeping early
examples private for now. The pattern is already clear: the best work happens
when the system makes the operator less dependent on memory and less dependent
on me.

# X Thread Draft

1/ I am building a small consulting practice around operator systems.

2/ Most small businesses do not need AI first. They need the business to stop
depending on one person's memory.

3/ The real unit is the operator: owner, spouse, assistant, coach, admin,
founder. Whoever keeps the day-to-day moving.

4/ The work starts with questions:
What breaks?
What gets forgotten?
What is manual for no good reason?
What should stay manual?
What needs to be handed off?

5/ Sometimes the answer is AI. Sometimes it is a better intake process, admin
portal, checklist, dashboard, or runbook.

6/ My internal system is repo-centered: skills, commands, fixtures, agent roles,
proof gates, and lessons that improve after real work.

7/ The goal is not SaaS. The goal is a capped practice that builds useful
systems and hands them off cleanly.

8/ The best proof is simple: the operator can run the system without me in the
middle.

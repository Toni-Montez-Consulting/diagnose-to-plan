# Business Brain Pitch Kit

## Audience: Coworkers / AI-Fluent Peers

### 30 Seconds

I am building a repo-centered operating system for a capped consulting practice.
The interesting part is not "AI consulting." It is the skill, agent, and
artifact layer that compounds across engagements and can be installed in a
client context without becoming SaaS.

### 60 Seconds

I am treating the consulting practice like a software system. The repo is HQ,
markdown is the protocol, agents write artifacts, the console renders them, and
Notion mirrors what humans need to see. The same structure handles meeting prep,
COI screens, proposals, handoffs, proof packets, lessons, and future skill
refactors against evals. It is a business-practice OS, not a chatbot wrapper.

### 2 Minutes

Most AI work I see small businesses talk about starts with tools. I am starting
with the operator: the person carrying scheduling, admin, sales, follow-up,
handoff, and all the weird business logic in their head. I am building a capped
practice around diagnosing that pressure, installing the smallest useful system,
and handing it off clearly. The Business Brain is how the practice improves:
skills, command contracts, fixtures, runbooks, evals, and human-reviewed
refactors live in the repo. Mario, Mom, Greg, Cameron, and Omnexus are early
proof threads. They test local-business admin, non-technical handoff,
founder/product clarity, equity/COI boundaries, and product shipping discipline.

## Audience: Friends / Family Outside Tech

### 30 Seconds

I help small business owners get important work out of their head and into a
system someone else can run.

### 60 Seconds

Some owners are doing everything from memory: leads, admin, scheduling,
follow-up, payments, decisions, handoff. I help them figure out what is actually
stuck, build or clean up the right system, and leave them with a plain-language
runbook. Sometimes that uses AI. Sometimes it is a better intake process,
admin portal, checklist, or assistant.

### 2 Minutes

The business is about ordinary operators. Think of someone running a coaching
business, family office process, nonprofit workflow, local service business, or
early product. The work is often not broken because they need one more app. It
is broken because too much is in one person's head. I come in, diagnose the
bottleneck, decide what should be fixed first, build a useful system, and hand
it off so the operator can actually run it. The Business Brain is my own system
for making that repeatable: meeting prep, proposals, runbooks, lessons, and
future improvements all live in the repo.

## Audience: Wife / Family

### 30 Seconds

This is a slow, capped consulting practice, not a chaotic side hustle. I am
using what I am learning to help real operators build systems they can run.

### 60 Seconds

The goal is not to take every client or turn this into a giant agency. The goal
is a small number of deep engagements where I can diagnose the real operating
problem, build the right thing, and hand it off cleanly. The system is designed
to protect boundaries: COI screens, no public proof without review, and no
open-ended founder commitments unless they are clearly scoped.

### 2 Minutes

I am building the practice carefully because the life shape matters. The work
uses my engineering judgment and daily AI exposure, but it is aimed at ordinary
operators: people like Mario or Mom who need a system that makes the business
less dependent on memory. I want the practice capped, evidence-based, and
disciplined. Every engagement should leave an artifact: a runbook, portal,
assistant, intake process, proposal, or operating kit. The repo keeps the
learning from turning into scattered notes.

## Audience: Business Professionals

### 30 Seconds

I run a capped consulting practice that installs operating systems for
owner-led businesses: diagnose the bottleneck, build the first useful system,
and hand off the operating rhythm.

### 60 Seconds

The model is paid diagnostic work, scoped builds, and support only after the
system is real. The practice is intentionally small. It uses AI and automation

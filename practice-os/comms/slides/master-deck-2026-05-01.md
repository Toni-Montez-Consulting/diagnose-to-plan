# Master Deck: Business Brain / Consulting OS

## Slide 1: The Simple Thesis

- Ordinary operators deserve serious operating systems.
- The work starts with the human running the business.
- AI helps only when it makes the system easier to operate.

Speaker note: Lead with the operator, not the tool.

## Slide 2: The Operator Problem

- Too much business logic lives in one person's head.
- Work is scattered across texts, calendars, apps, memory, and follow-up.
- The business breaks when the operator is busy or away.

Speaker note: Use Mario/Mom privately as the human example.

## Slide 3: What This Is

- Diagnose the bottleneck.
- Build the first useful system.
- Hand off the operating rhythm.

Speaker note: Keep the promise clear and concrete.

## Slide 4: What This Is Not

- Not a generic AI automation agency.
- Not SaaS.
- Not a course, cohort, or content business.

Speaker note: This protects the shape of the practice.

## Slide 5: The Four Offer Shapes

- Diagnostic / AI Upgrade Audit.
- Operating System install.
- Assistant or Workflow Kit.
- Launch Sprint as the Builder-path exception.

Speaker note: Do not sell all four equally to every audience.

## Slide 6: The Business Brain

- Repo is HQ.
- Skills, commands, agents, fixtures, comms, runbooks, evals.
- Humans approve changes.

Speaker note: For technical audiences, this is the interesting architecture.

## Slide 7: How It Runs

- DTP owns Practice OS.
- Hub supports runtime intake.
- Notion mirrors selected human-facing items.

Speaker note: Notion is not source of truth.

## Slide 8: Proof Threads

- Mario/Mom: operator handoff.
- Greg: founder/product case-study shape.
- Cameron: Builder-path boundary and COI.
- Omnexus: shipped-product discipline.

Speaker note: Private proof points; public use needs review.

## Slide 9: Compliance Discipline

- COI before contracting.
- Permission before public proof.
- Redaction before case studies.

Speaker note: This is part of the product, not paperwork after the fact.

## Slide 10: The Handoff

- Runbook.
- Walkthrough.
- What can change.
- What not to touch.

Speaker note: The client should be less dependent on Toni after the build.

## Slide 11: The Compounding Loop

- Work creates artifacts.
- Artifacts create lessons.
- Lessons become candidates.
- Humans review updates.

Speaker note: Self-improvement is supervised.

## Slide 12: The Ask

- Bring the messy version.
- Name what breaks.
- Start with the first useful system.

Speaker note: End with a conversation, not a pitch for a platform.

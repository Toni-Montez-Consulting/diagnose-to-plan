## Current Interpretation

- What this really means: Questions are part of the collaboration mechanism,
  not only a blocker-resolution tool. For meaningful strategy/build work, Codex
  should make its thinking legible, ask useful questions, and then build.
- Why it matters: Toni uses the questions to learn the system, understand where
  the work is going, refine ambition, and keep the output aligned with the
  consulting/business direction.
- What it could improve: Fewer silent assumptions, less feeling of Codex
  running ahead, better shared taste, better sequencing, and stronger durable
  artifacts.
- What should not be inferred: This does not mean stopping for questions on
  tiny mechanical tasks, command output requests, obvious fixes, or urgent
  blockers where the safe next step is clear.

# Authentic Voice And Anti-Slop Standard

Use this standard for public copy, client emails, proof packets, assistant
answers, roadmap language, planning artifacts, and any document that explains
what Toni builds.

The north star:

> A serious person explaining what actually happened.

The goal is not monotone writing. The goal is clear, specific, human, and
earned writing. Style is allowed when the sentence still says something a
knowledgeable reader can trust.

## What This Prevents

Do not ship language that sounds polished but does not survive inspection.

Blocked patterns:

- Generic AI-agency positioning.
- Fake transformation language.
- Punchy slogans that do not name the mechanism.
- Over-polished case-study phrasing before proof exists.
- Claims that imply permission, results, metrics, launch state, or client
  approval that has not been recorded.
- Prose that sounds better than it means.
- Certainty reconstructed from memory without a current source check.
- Vague proof language that hides missing evidence, caveats, redaction, or
  reviewer status.

## Preferred Pattern

Prefer sentences that name:

- the real pressure;
- the workflow or system that changed;
- the evidence source;
- the before-state and after-state;
- what stayed manual on purpose;
- the caveat;
- the next action.

Good copy can be warm and direct. It should not sound like a funnel.

## Phrase Watchlist

These phrases are not always forbidden in quoted source text or technical
contexts, but they require review before public/proof-facing use:

- AI-powered
- AI-driven
- future-proof
- 10x
- transform your
- cutting-edge
- unlock
- revolutionary
- game-changing
- seamless AI
- scale overnight
- leverage AI

If a phrase is kept, the reviewer must be able to point to the concrete
mechanism and evidence behind it.

## Rewrite Examples

Weak:

> We unlock AI-powered operational transformation for modern teams.

Better:

> We mapped the intake path, removed the manual handoff, and left the owner
> with a record someone else can follow.

Weak:

> This intelligence layer creates a new paradigm for business execution.

Better:

> This shows active client lanes, blockers, proof status, and the next decision
> Toni needs to make.

Weak:

> A complete proof system for any client.

Better:

> A proof packet can move forward only after evidence, permission, caveat,
> redaction status, and reviewer are recorded.

## Review Questions

Before publishing, sending, or promoting an artifact, ask:

- Would Toni actually say this to a serious owner?
- Would a knowledgeable reader believe this claim?
- Does the sentence name the mechanism, not just the aspiration?
- Is this supported by source material, current repo state, or recorded
  permission?
- Does this hide a manual gate?
- Could this belong to any AI consultant? If yes, make it more specific or cut
  it.
- Is the caveat visible enough?
- Is the next action plain?

## Decision Rule

- Public/proof-facing copy with unsupported claims is blocked.
- Internal planning copy with vague language should be rewritten before it
  becomes a roadmap, receipt, template, or public artifact.
- Client emails should be short, specific, and owner-friendly; do not make them
  sound like marketing automation.
- Assistant answers should state what is known, what is inferred, and what is
  still open instead of filling gaps with confident prose.

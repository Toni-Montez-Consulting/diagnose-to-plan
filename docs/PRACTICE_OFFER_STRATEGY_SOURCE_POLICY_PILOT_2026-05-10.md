## Strategy Decision

Keep the public front door broad.

Do not lead publicly with "AI consulting" as the main category. Lead with the
broader owner/operator problem:

> unclear ideas, broken workflows, and tech bottlenecks need to become
> practical systems that work.

AI should appear as a practical implementation capability inside the system:

- sometimes AI belongs in the client-facing workflow;
- sometimes AI belongs behind the scenes in the delivery process;
- sometimes the workflow, source material, ownership, or review boundary needs
  to be fixed before AI is worth building.

The offer language should say, in effect:

> I help figure out where AI belongs in the work, then build the operating
> asset around it.

## Offer Implications

### 1. Business Systems Blueprint

This is the default route when the prospect says:

- "I want AI but do not know where it belongs."
- "My workflow is messy and I think AI could help."
- "We have repeated manual work, but no clear implementation plan."
- "I need to understand value, risk, and scope before spending on a build."

Blueprint should answer:

- What workflow is actually being improved?
- Where is the work slow, unclear, repeated, or dependent on owner judgment?
- What source material or data exists?
- Who reviews AI-assisted output?
- What should stay human-owned?
- What is the smallest useful implementation?
- What should not be built yet?

AI-specific Blueprint outputs can include:

- workflow map;
- source/data inventory;
- AI opportunity score;
- human-review boundary;
- risk and dependency register;
- simple technical path;
- success metrics;
- build / no-build recommendation;
- recommended SOW route.

### 2. Fast Track Implementation

Fast Track can fit AI work only when the use case is already clear.

Use when:

- the workflow is narrow;
- source material is available;
- ownership and review are clear;
- the implementation can be scoped without deep discovery;
- risk is low enough to avoid a full Blueprint.

Examples:

- AI-assisted intake summary for a known workflow;
- internal drafting helper with human review;
- simple routing or classification step;
- prompt/workflow kit for a repeated admin task;
- small AI-assisted dashboard or operating note.

Do not use Fast Track when:

- the client cannot explain the workflow;
- the source material is messy or sensitive;
- legal, financial, health, privacy, or compliance risk is high;
- the client wants a chatbot or automation with no operating model.

### 3. Custom Implementation SOW

Custom Implementation follows an approved Blueprint or clear Fast Track scope.

AI-enabled Custom SOWs should carry:

- source/data boundaries;
- human review points;
- acceptance criteria;
- logging or audit expectations when needed;
- handoff/training;
- operating runbook;
- kill switch or rollback path for risky workflows.

### 4. Growth, AI, And Operating Systems

This lane is for existing businesses that want leverage, modernization, and
better operations.

Do not sell it as "AI transformation." Sell it as:

> find the workflow, AI, and operating-system leverage that can reduce drag and
> make the business easier to run.

This lane can produce:

- growth/efficiency opportunity map;
- AI/workflow opportunity score;
- owner/admin rhythm;
- command-room fit assessment;
- implementation roadmap;
- optimization backlog.

## Buyer-Fit Filter

Strong fit:

- serious owner or founder;
- real workflow pain;
- budget for paid discovery or implementation;
- willingness to expose enough context to diagnose the work;
- someone owns the decision;
- someone will review AI-assisted output;
- outcome can be described in business terms;
- client wants implementation, not just ideas.

Weak fit:

- wants "AI" because it sounds current;
- wants a chatbot with no workflow behind it;
- no source material or process owner;
- no human-review path;
- no budget or urgency;
- expects AI to replace judgment;
- wants strategy-only advice with no implementation path;
- high-risk regulated work without review, counsel, controls, or budget.

## Offer Language Candidates

Internal only:

- Practical AI implementation for real business workflows.
- Figure out where AI belongs in the work, then build the system around it.
- AI should make the workflow clearer and easier to operate.
- We map the workflow first, then decide where AI can reduce drag.
- Human review stays built into the system.
- The goal is not to add AI everywhere. The goal is to make the business work
  better.

Avoid:

- AI transformation;
- AI-powered growth;
- autonomous agent workforce;
- turnkey AI system;
- 10x efficiency;
- chatbot-first language;
- any claim that OpenAI, Microsoft, or another source validates Toni's offer.

## Public-Site Implication

No public site change is approved by this pilot.

When the public-copy pass reopens, the safest source-backed change is not a new
AI offer page. It is a clearer AI posture inside existing sections:

- Hero/subhead: practical AI/workflow implementation stays broad and
  implementation-first.
- Blueprint bridge: unclear AI questions route to Blueprint.
- Implementation promise: AI is used where it creates leverage, with human
  review and business judgment in the process.
- `/start`: keep the AI/workflow opportunity option and ask what currently
  causes friction.
- Bad-fit filter: exclude chatbot-for-chatbot's-sake and random automations
  with no operating model.

## Decision Tree

| Prospect signal | Best route | Reason |
|---|---|---|
| "I have an idea and think AI could help." | Fit Call -> Blueprint | idea, workflow, data, and value need definition first |
| "We repeat this manual task every week and know who reviews it." | Fit Call -> Fast Track if scope is narrow | enough clarity may exist to build a contained implementation |
| "Our operation is scattered and we want AI leverage." | Fit Call -> Blueprint -> Custom SOW | workflow and operating model need diagnosis before build |
| "Can you add a chatbot to my site?" | Fit Call only if there is a real workflow; otherwise not-fit | chatbot alone is not the offer |
| "We need AI to make decisions for us." | likely not-fit or gated review | human judgment and review boundaries are required |
| "We need a working system and know what it should do." | Fit Call -> Fast Track or Custom SOW | scope clarity can shorten discovery |
